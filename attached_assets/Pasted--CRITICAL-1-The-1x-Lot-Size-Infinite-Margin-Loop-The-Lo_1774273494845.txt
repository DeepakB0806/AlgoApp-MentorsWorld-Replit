🛑 CRITICAL 1: The "1x Lot Size" Infinite Margin Loop
The Loophole: In your executeLegBasket retry loop, the new lot size is calculated as: Math.max(1, ctx.lotMultiplier - attempt).
If a user is trading with a lotMultiplier of 1, and Kotak rejects the order for margin, the engine rolls back the trade and tries again. However, because of the Math.max(1, ...) floor, the next attempt will also be at a 1x multiplier.
The Danger: It will endlessly retry the exact same 1x lot size, fail for margin, and market-sell the rollback, bleeding your account dry via brokerage fees and slippage on the successful leg until maxRetries runs out.

The Fix: The loop must cleanly abort if the multiplier hits the floor and a margin error occurs.

🛑 CRITICAL 2: Partial Fills Breaking the Exit Engine
The Loophole:
Kotak sometimes partially fills market orders (e.g., you order 100 lots, but only 50 get filled due to liquidity). Currently, executeLegBasket reads the actual filled quantity via getFillPrice, but it saves resolved.quantity (the intended 100 lots) to the database.
The Danger: When the exit signal arrives, your engine will try to SELL 100 lots. Kotak will instantly reject this order for "Insufficient Holdings" (because you only own 50). The trade goes into close_failed, and the persistent retry loop will hammer Kotak endlessly trying to sell 100 lots, getting rejected every time.

The Fix: The engine must save the actual filled quantity to the database.

⚠️ HIGH 3: Rollback Order API Failure (The Permanent Ghost)
The Loophole:
When a margin shortfall occurs, the engine fires an Atomic Rollback order to market-sell the orphaned leg. But what if Kotak's API times out during the rollback request?
The Danger: Currently, the engine just loops through the rollback placeOrder without checking the result. If the rollback fails, the ghost trade remains in your broker account, but the engine thinks it was cleanly aborted.

The Fix: We must check the rollback response and fire a FATAL Process Flow Log alert if it fails, so you know manual intervention is required on your Kotak app.

⚠️ HIGH 4: "Reboot Amnesia" for Persistent Retries
The Loophole:
startPersistentExit and startPersistentSquareOff use Node.js setTimeout and in-memory Set objects to keep retrying failed exits.
The Danger: If your Replit server restarts, crashes, or goes to sleep, those memory timers are destroyed. Any trade stuck in close_failed will remain stuck forever because the engine forgot it was trying to close them.

The Fix: We need a "Boot-up Catcher" that queries the database for close_failed trades when the server starts and re-ignites the retry loops automatically.
