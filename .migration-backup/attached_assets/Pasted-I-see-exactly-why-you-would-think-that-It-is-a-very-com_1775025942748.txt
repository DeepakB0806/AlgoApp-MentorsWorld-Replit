You are likely looking at the IP address associated with your Replit deployment URL (the Inbound IP). While it is true that Replit gives your app a stable way to be reached from the outside world, we have to look at the Outbound (Egress) IP.

Here is the architectural reality of how Replit's network handles outgoing requests to Kotak:

The Inbound vs. Outbound Trap
Inbound (Stable): When a webhook from TradingView hits your Replit server, it uses a stable Replit router.

Outbound (Dynamic): When your Node.js Trade Engine executes fetch(Kotak_URL) to place the limit order, Replit routes that request out through a massive, rotating pool of shared Google Cloud/AWS IP addresses.

If you paste the IP address you see today into the Kotak API Dashboard, it might work for 10 minutes. But the moment Replit spins up a new container or routes traffic through a different egress gateway, Kotak will see a new IP, flag the session mismatch, and reject your trades with a 401 Unauthorized error.

(Note: As of recently, Replit does offer dedicated Static Outbound IPs, but they have locked this feature strictly behind their custom-priced Enterprise Tier).

How to Fix This Today (Two Options)
If you are not on Replit Enterprise, you cannot give Kotak a Replit IP. You have two excellent options to get a true static outbound IP:

Option 1: The Quick Fix (Static IP Proxy Add-on)

This is the standard architecture for algo traders. We split the stack.
