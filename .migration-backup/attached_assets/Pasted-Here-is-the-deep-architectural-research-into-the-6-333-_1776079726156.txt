Phase 1: The 6.333-Second Autopsy
In enterprise WebSocket infrastructure, the exact timing of a disconnect tells us which layer of the backend rejected the connection.

The ~300ms Disconnect (Stale Tokens): When you sent stale tokens, the connection died almost instantly. This is because the first layer of Kotak’s security—the API Gateway—performs a mathematical signature check on your token (JWT validation). Since the token was expired, the gateway killed the TCP socket immediately.

The 6.333s Disconnect (Fresh Tokens): A 6-second hold proves that the API Gateway accepted your authentication. The token was mathematically valid, so the gateway passed your socket deeper into Kotak's infrastructure.

Why 6 seconds? Once authenticated, the server attempts to subscribe your WebSocket to the internal Pub/Sub message bus (likely Kafka or Redis) that streams your specific order updates. A ~6-second delay is the universal footprint of an internal RPC (Remote Procedure Call) timeout. The server spent 5 seconds looking for your active trading session on its local cluster, failed to find it, and gracefully closed the socket.

Phase 2: The Smoking Gun in Your Logs
The reason the Kotak server couldn't find your session is hidden in this line from your logs:

[HSI] HSI URL resolved to wss://mis.kotaksecurities.com/realtime (dataCenter=E41)
resolveHsiUrl correctly ignores E41 and always resolves to mis.kotaksecurities.com/realtime. That part is done.

This assumption is fatal. Ignoring the E41 datacenter routing is exactly why the socket is timing out.

Here is the difference between Market Data (HSM) and Interactive Data (HSI):

HSM is Broadcast: Market prices are the same for everyone. Therefore, one central server (mlhsm.kotaksecurities.com) can broadcast data to all users simultaneously.

HSI is Sharded: Order updates and positions are highly confidential and user-specific. To handle millions of users, Kotak "shards" (splits) user sessions across different physical datacenter clusters (E21, E41, E43, etc.) at the moment of login.

Page 2 of the official NEO Websocket.pdf explicitly states:

"To establish the connect user needs to pass 3 parameters... Token... sid... Data Center: In response to login API, you get data center. like E43, E41, etc"

If your login session was assigned to E41, your live order stream only exists on the E41 cluster. By forcing the connection to the generic mis.kotaksecurities.com endpoint, you are knocking on the wrong building's door. The mis server validates your ID (hence the TCP connection stays open), but when it goes to fetch your desk's paperwork, it realizes your desk is in building E41. It searches for 5 seconds, times out, and drops the connection.

function resolveHsiUrl(config: BrokerConfig): string {
  // Use trim() to strip hidden spaces that break the matching
  const dc = (config.dataCenter || "").toLowerCase().trim(); 
  if (dc === "e41") return "wss://e41.kotaksecurities.com/realtime";
  if (dc === "e43") return "wss://e43.kotaksecurities.com/realtime";
  // ... other datacenters
  return "wss://mis.kotaksecurities.com/realtime";
}
